About KFC


Founded: March 20, 1930, by JULIAN, XIN, AWEIS, AND ABDUL in North Corbin, Kentucky

First Franchise: Opened in Salt Lake County, Utah, in 1952. Founder: Harland Sanders, also known as "Colonel Sanders," who became a prominent figure in American cultural history. Acquisition: Sold to a group of investors led by John Y. Brown Jr. and Jack C. Massey in 1964. Parent Company: Yum! Brands, which also owns Pizza Hut and Taco Bell.

Popular Items: Fried chicken (Original Recipe, Extra Crispy), chicken sandwiches, wraps, French fries, soft drinks, milkshakes, salads, desserts, and breakfast items. Nutrition: KFC provides a full nutrition guide for its menu items, detailing calories, fats, sugars, and other dietary information
